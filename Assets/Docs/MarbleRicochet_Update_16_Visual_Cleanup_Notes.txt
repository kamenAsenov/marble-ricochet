
MARBLE RICOCHET — VISUAL CLEANUP UPDATE 16

Brutally critical visual analysis:
1) The biggest visual problem was not “lack of polish”, but visual noise.
   The warm yellow/orange lines across the whole board made the game feel like a wireframe prototype.
   Too many bright lines meant the eye noticed the board decoration more than the gameplay objects.

2) Why it still didn’t feel premium:
   - The brown/warm board language still leaked through.
   - Too much line detail for a mobile screen.
   - UI hierarchy was messy: hints, ability text, hazard hint and fail text all competed.
   - The fail overlay still felt flat and blocky instead of clean and premium.

3) What changed in Update 16:
   - Removed the full-board line noise.
   - Replaced it with a clean graphite playfield and premium frame-led design.
   - Kept only minimal launch guidance near the bottom.
   - Improved panel/card styling and button styling.
   - Tidied text placement and reduced top-of-screen clutter.
   - Refined hazard visuals to feel cleaner and more elegant.

Visual direction:
Minimal premium tabletop.
Palette:
- matte black
- soft graphite
- satin brass
- subtle cyan accents
- muted ruby hazards

Why this is better:
This follows strong dark-theme principles: limited accents, clear hierarchy, good contrast, and fewer competing colors. Android’s design guidance recommends consistent color roles and avoiding too many mixed colors in one view. Apple’s HIG for color/dark appearances also emphasizes adaptive color and good contrast. Unity’s mobile optimization guidance favors simple shader/material setups over overly expensive effects. How to install:
1) Stop Play Mode.
2) Unzip package.
3) Copy Assets folder over the Unity project Assets folder.
4) Choose merge/replace.
5) Wait for Unity to compile.
6) Run:
   Tools > Marble Ricochet > Apply Visual Cleanup Update 16
7) OK.
8) Play.
