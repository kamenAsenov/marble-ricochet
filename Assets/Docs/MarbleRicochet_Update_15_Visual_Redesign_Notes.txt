MARBLE RICOCHET — VISUAL REDESIGN UPDATE 15

Chosen art direction:
Graphite Luxury Tabletop.

Palette:
- void black / deep graphite background
- smoked graphite playing surface
- matte black rubber rails
- brushed brass accents
- icy cyan gameplay/glass accents
- ruby hazard accents

Why this direction:
It removes the cheap brown / childish table feel while keeping the game readable. It is more premium than cartoon wood, less risky than full neon sci-fi, and more visually attractive on mobile screenshots.

Brutal visual analysis:
1) What made it look prototype-like:
- Too much muddy brown.
- Table looked like stacked primitive shapes.
- Some colors felt added feature-by-feature, not art-directed.
- UI panels and table materials were not part of one visual system.

2) What was pulling it down:
- Brown surface lacked realism and taste.
- Gold/cyan/hazard colors were not consistently balanced.
- Lighting was functional but not cinematic enough.

3) What felt childish:
- Table color and object contrast felt toy-like.
- Rails and surface did not feel like premium materials.
- UI buttons felt generic.

4) What was missing:
- A cohesive color palette.
- Clear material roles.
- Visual hierarchy: table subdued, gameplay accents bright, hazards distinct.

5) What not to do:
- No clown colors.
- No neon overload.
- No everything-brown look.
- No visual clutter.
- No sacrificing readability for beauty.

Three directions considered:
A) Premium modern tabletop:
Graphite/smoked surface, brass accents, icy glass. High readability, strong App Store look, low performance risk.

B) Futuristic luxury puzzle:
Gunmetal and neon cyan. Very crisp but risk of cheap neon overload if pushed too far.

C) Minimal stone/marble:
Slate and marble gallery look. Elegant but could become too cold and less readable.

Selected:
A, with a controlled touch of B — Graphite Luxury Tabletop.

How to install:
1) Stop Play Mode.
2) Unzip package.
3) Copy Assets folder over the Unity project's Assets folder.
4) Choose merge/replace.
5) Wait for Unity to compile.
6) Run:
   Tools > Marble Ricochet > Apply Visual Redesign Update 15
7) OK.
8) Play.

What to test:
1) Does the table no longer look cheap brown?
2) Does it feel more premium and realistic?
3) Are rails still clear?
4) Are holes/hazards readable?
5) Is the UI cleaner and more consistent?
6) Are glass targets still visible?
7) Is the game readable on phone?
8) Any Console errors?
